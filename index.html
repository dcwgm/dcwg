About the Event

Welcome to the Disaster Crisis Working Group Meeting in Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia! This event brings together experts and stakeholders to discuss disaster management strategies and foster collaboration.

Arrival Information

Travel Restrictions

Mongolia fully opened its air borders to international travel on February 14, 2022. No negative PCR or rapid test results are required to enter Mongolia before departure. No testing is required upon arrival at the border points. No quarantine or self-isolation is required.

Before Arrival

All visitors to Mongolia must hold a valid passport with a minimum validity of 6 months from the date of entry. If a visa is required, visitors must have a valid visa or entry permission letter for entry into Mongolia.

Upon Arrival

Chinggis Khaan International Airport is the second largest airport in Mongolia, featuring six terminals. Please follow the signs for immigration and baggage claim. Mongolian Red Cross Society staff will be waiting to assist with airport pick-up.

Venue

The meeting will be held at the Mongolian Red Cross Society building:

Address: The International Youth Training Center is located 68 km east of Ulaanbaatar city in Erdene soum of Tuv province. Set in 9 acres of beautiful land, the training center is just 7km from the largest horse-riding statue in the world, the Chinggis Khaan Statue, one of the most visited tourist attraction sites in Mongolia

Transportation

Getting to and from the venue:

  • From Chinggis Khaan International Airport: Taxi fares range from 100,000 to 150,000 MNT. Pre-booked taxis are recommended.
  • Public Transport: Bus fare is 10,000 MNT for adults and 5,000 MNT for children. Routes include Bus line 1 and Bus line 2.

Discover Ulaanbaatar

Explore some of Ulaanbaatar's most iconic attractions:

  • Gandan Temple: A significant Buddhist site and one of the greatest temples in Mongolia.
  • Parliament House: A striking landmark combining traditional and modern architecture.
  • Zaisan Hill Memorial: Offers panoramic views of the city and commemorates Soviet soldiers who died in World War II.
  • Khustain Nuru National Park: Home to the wild Takhi horse (Przewalski's horse).
  • Chinggis Khaan Museum: Learn about the history of Chinggis Khaan and Mongolian culture.
